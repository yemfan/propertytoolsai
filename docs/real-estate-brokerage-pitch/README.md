# Real Estate Brokerage Pitch — Asset Index

Everything needed to sell LeadSmart AI to an independent real estate brokerage (30–200 agents) as a year-one license commitment.

**Buyer:** brokerage owner / managing broker.
**Ask:** paid annual license, per-agent pricing.
**Positioning:** the operating system for running a brokerage — not another CRM.

## How to use this for a brokerage sales meeting

### One week before
1. Send the **one-pager** + the **ROI calculator** scaled to their estimated agent count.
2. Read the **objection FAQ** end-to-end. Mark the 3 questions most likely for this brokerage's profile (franchise vs. independent, urban vs. rural, single-office vs. multi).

### Two days before
3. Read the **demo script** end-to-end. Open the live workspace URLs. Make sure the broker dashboard renders cleanly.
4. Pre-generate a CMA for a real address in their market so the cache warms.

### Day of
5. Open with the **hook** in Scene 0 — *speak before sharing screen.*
6. Lead with the **broker dashboard**, not the agent CRM. The buyer is the broker, not the agent.
7. Pivot to the **CMA generator** as the wow moment. It's the agent-adoption wedge.
8. End with concrete pricing for their agent count and an explicit **45-min working session** ask.

### After
9. Within 24 hours, send the follow-up email with the one-pager, the ROI calculator scaled to their numbers, and any specific questions they raised.
10. If they want references, be honest about the first-mover framing.

## Quick reference — the four metrics that move

These are the numbers the brokerage owner sees on their P&L. Each one maps to a specific LeadSmart AI mechanism.

| Metric | Mechanism in the product |
|---|---|
| Median speed-to-lead under 5 min | AI SMS nurture in the agent's voice, fires within 5 min of inbound |
| Lead → appointment conversion +50% | AI-drafted persistent follow-up; CMAs delivered with appointment invites |
| Agents under 6 sides / yr: −15 pp | AI nurture handles 80% of follow-up the underperformers were skipping; the system makes them look competent |
| Agent retention +10 pp | Agent-branded marketing tools (home-value funnel, CMAs) — top producers stay when their broker gives them tools that make them look good |

## What we're still asking the brokerage to do

If the meeting goes well, the immediate ask is:

1. **45-minute working session** with the owner + managing broker + 1–2 top producers
2. **Their actual numbers** for the ROI model — current lead spend, average GCI per side, current retention rate, current avg sides per agent
3. **Verbal commitment** to a 12-month license at the close of the working session, contingent on the ROI model checking out against their numbers
4. **Designated executive sponsor** named at signing (owner, managing broker, or COO)

That's the deal shape. Nothing else.
