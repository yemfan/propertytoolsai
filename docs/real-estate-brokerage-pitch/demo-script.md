# Demo Script — Brokerage Owner Walkthrough

> **Audience:** Brokerage owner + managing broker, sometimes one or two top producers in the room.
> **Target time:** 12 min demo + 3–5 min Q&A.
> **Ask at the end:** A 45-min working session with their actual numbers, leading to a year-one license commitment.
>
> Structured as 7 scenes with timing, navigation, talk track, and Q&A branches. The talk track is verbatim-friendly — internalize, don't read.

---

## Pre-demo setup (15 min before the call)

1. Pre-open:
   - `https://leadsmart-ai.com/` (marketing landing — start here)
   - Signed-in dashboard at `https://leadsmart-ai.com/dashboard/broker` (broker view)
   - Agent dashboard at `https://leadsmart-ai.com/dashboard/overview` (agent view)
   - `https://leadsmart-ai.com/dashboard/cma` (CMA generator — the wedge)
2. **Pre-generate one CMA** for a sample address. Cached responses return in <2 sec instead of 60.
3. Browser zoom 110–125%. Close everything else.
4. Notepad open for objections, commitments, and next-step asks.

## Scene 0 · 30 sec · The hook (before opening anything)

**Don't share screen yet.** Look at them.

> "Before I show you anything — here's the bet. Brokerages compete on two numbers: average GCI per agent and 12-month retention. Most brokerage tech treats the broker as a passive bystander. The agent gets the CRM, the broker gets a quarterly report. We built this with the broker as the primary user. Twelve minutes — let me show you what that looks like."

**Then share screen.**

## Scene 1 · 90 sec · Broker dashboard — the command center

**Navigate:** `/dashboard/broker`

**Point at (in this order):**
1. Brokerage-wide KPI strip — total pipeline, this-month closes, speed-to-lead median, retention rate
2. Real-time agent leaderboard — sortable by speed-to-lead, response rate, appointments-set, closes
3. Agent-fitness flags panel — "Sarah's response time slipped to 4h+ last week" / "Mike has 3 hot leads gone cold > 48h" / "Lisa's pipeline is shrinking week-over-week"
4. Lead-distribution rules — round-robin / geo / top-producer / opt-in toggles

**Talk track:**

> "Here's the broker's home base. Not the agent's — the broker's. Top of the screen, the four numbers that determine whether this brokerage is growing or stalling: total pipeline value, this month's closes, the speed-to-lead median across all 100 agents, and your 12-month retention rate.
>
> Below that — the leaderboard. Every agent, ranked by the things you actually care about. Speed-to-lead, response rate, appointments-set, closes. Visible to all agents. Top producers compete for the top spot. Underperformers can't hide. You don't have to call team meetings to ask who's converting — you open this page.
>
> And this — the fitness-flags panel — is the killer feature. It's not waiting for you to ask. It's telling you 'Sarah's response time slipped this week. Mike has three hot leads going cold. Lisa's pipeline is shrinking.' These are the early signals of an agent about to under-produce — or quit. We catch them before you do."

>
> Watch this."

**Click "Generate CMA."** (Pre-warmed cache — returns in 1-2 seconds.)

**Talk track (after render):**

> "Sixty seconds. Agent-branded. Real comps from the MLS. Pricing recommendation, market trend analysis, ready to send. Agent can edit, then one-click email or text to the homeowner.
>
> This is the moment your agents look up and go 'wait, this is actually useful.' The agent who walks into the listing appointment tomorrow with this in their hand closes at a different rate than the one who comes empty-handed."

## Q&A branches — anticipate these

### "How is this different from kvCORE / FUB / Chime?"

> "kvCORE was built for the agent first, broker is a reporting afterthought. FUB has no broker tools at all. Chime is closer but their AI is mostly templates. The big structural difference is we built the broker dashboard as a first-class product — the leaderboard, the fitness flags, the lead-routing rules — those aren't bolt-ons in our system, they're the home screen."

### "Will my agents actually adopt it? We've tried switching CRMs before."

> "Adoption is the real game. Two answers. One: we don't make the agent learn a new CRM from scratch — the AI runs in the background of their existing workflow, drafting responses they review and send. The friction is closer to 'check email' than 'learn Salesforce.' Two: the home-value funnel and CMA generator are tools agents will use *because they want to look professional*, not because the broker told them to. Adoption follows usefulness."

### "What about MLS / IDX integration?"

> "We integrate with most major MLS via standard RETS / Spark feeds. If your MLS is on the supported list, that's day-one. If it's custom, we add it during onboarding — typically two weeks. We don't replace your IDX vendor; we sit beside it."

### "What about migration from our current CRM?"

> "Standard CSV import for contacts, leads, pipeline stages. Workflow translation we do as part of onboarding — your existing tags, sources, statuses get mapped manually. Plan for 4–6 weeks of parallel running where both CRMs work, then deprecate the old one. We've done this."

### "How long until we see results?"

> "Speed-to-lead and lead-conversion lift show up in the first 30 days — those are the easy ones. Retention and producer-ratio shifts take 6–9 months to measure. Our license includes a 30-day onboarding checkpoint: if cohort retention isn't above 50% at day 30, we restructure the contract together."

### "What if we want to leave?"

> "Year-one annual contract. Month-to-month after. Your data exports to CSV / JSON in one click. We don't lock anything behind tiers, don't charge for export, don't slow-walk you out. If we can't earn the renewal, you should leave."

### "Has any brokerage with 100+ agents used this?"

> "Honestly, you'd be among our first three large-brokerage deployments. 3,400+ producers on the platform total, but most are at the agent or small-team tier. That's the tradeoff: in exchange for being first-mover, you get the product shaped around your workflow, direct founder access during deployment, and a case study at month 12 that becomes your moat against the brokerages who come after."

### "Pricing seems aggressive — what's the catch?"

> "Not a catch. We're newer at the brokerage tier and pricing accordingly to win deployments. Pricing locks for year one. Year-two renewal could move, but we'll never move more than 10% in a single renewal cycle."

## Closing posture

If they say **"let's do it"** → confirm next steps: working session this week, exec sponsor + managing broker + 2 producers, ROI model on their numbers.

If they say **"send us the proposal in writing"** → confirm: "I'll have the contract draft in your inbox within 24 hours. Anything specific you need before signing — references, security review, compliance docs?"

If they say **"let me think about it"** → confirm: "Want me to send the one-pager + ROI illustration for [their agent count] over email so you have something concrete to share internally?"

If they say **"not for us"** → "Appreciate the time. Mind if I ask what the deal-breaker was?" Write down what they say. That's the most valuable thing you'll hear all month.

---

## Things NOT to do

- **Don't lead with AI features.** Lead with the broker dashboard. AI is the *how*, broker performance is the *what*.
- **Don't dwell on agent-level CRM.** The buyer doesn't care about the agent UI; they care about brokerage performance.
- **Don't promise dates for migration that depend on their data.** Migration time varies. Commit to a process, not a date.
- **Don't undersell on pricing.** Replacing kvCORE for half the cost is the story. Lean into it.
- **Don't apologize for being newer to the brokerage tier.** Frame it as opportunity — first-mover advantage, custom workflow shaping, founder access.
