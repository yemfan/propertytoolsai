# Objection FAQ — GFI Executive Pitch

> **How to use this doc.** These are the 10 questions GFI executives are most likely to ask in an informal 1-on-1, plus 2 MLM-specific bonus questions. Each entry has:
> - **Short answer** — what comes out of your mouth (2–3 sentences, verbatim-friendly).
> - **If they press** — paragraph-level detail you only use if the exec digs in.
> - **Don't say** — landmines to avoid.
>
> The goal isn't to "win" every question. It's to sound honest, prepared, and like someone who's thought harder about their objection than they have.

---

## 1. "How is this different from what our producers already use?"

**Short answer.** Most producers stack carrier portals + spreadsheets + WhatsApp + maybe AgencyBloc or Redtail. None of those were built for the *pre-sale and recruiting* layer — they assume the prospect is already ready to apply. LeadSmart AI sits *before* the carrier portal: it finds leads, nurtures them in under five minutes with AI, and produces the FNA that drives the kitchen-table sit.

**If they press.** Carrier tools (TransACT, WinFlex, iPipeline) are excellent at illustrations, e-app, and policy management — and we don't try to replace any of that. Producers keep using them for what they're good at. AgencyBloc and Redtail are CRMs built for traditional independent agents — they don't model your hierarchy, don't generate FNAs, and don't have an AI nurture engine out of the box. Salesforce FSC is too expensive and too heavy for field producers. The wedge is: we make the first five minutes after lead capture, and the 60 seconds before a kitchen-table appointment, dramatically faster and more professional.

**Don't say.** "We replace your carrier portal." (We don't — they'll know that's a lie.) "AgencyBloc is bad." (Some of their producers probably love it.) Stay specific about *what we do that the existing stack doesn't*.

## 2. "What's the catch on the free pilot?"

**Short answer.** No catch. We're early in this vertical and we need real-world data from an agency that runs at scale. You're getting 90 days of platform access; we're getting honest metrics and a case study at the end. If the pilot fails, we walk away — no obligation either side.

**If they press.** Specifically: we cover platform access, onboarding, compliance review of templates, weekly metric reports, and a direct Slack channel with the founder. You provide a designated MD, a cohort of 10–25 producers, baseline metrics, and a 30-min weekly check-in. The "catch" — if you want to call it that — is we ask for permission to publish anonymized outcome metrics post-pilot. That's it. We're not asking for equity, exclusivity, or contractual lock-in for the pilot phase.

**Don't say.** "We're losing money on this." (Sounds desperate.) "We need you to commit to expanding after." (Defeats the point of a pilot.)

## 3. "Who reviews the AI-drafted messages — and what about TCPA, 17a-4, state suitability?"

**Short answer.** Every outbound AI-drafted message can be queued for principal or OSJ review before send — that's a configurable workflow per agency. Templates themselves get reviewed once at pilot start, not per-send. TCPA opt-in is logged before any SMS goes out. Communications are written to an audit-ready archive — that satisfies the spirit of FINRA 17a-4 / SEC 4511 retention, and we integrate with Smarsh or Global Relay for firms that mandate certified archiving.

**If they press.** State-specific disclosures are auto-appended by product and jurisdiction — your producer doesn't have to remember Florida's annuity suitability disclosure or California's life insurance free-look language. The system also blocks comms from producers whose state license is expired. Suitability questionnaires are stored, signed, and retrievable. For the pilot, we'd want your principal or OSJ to review the template library in week 1 — that's a one-time effort, maybe 5 business days, and after that producers can send freely within the approved library.

**Don't say.** "Compliance is handled" (vague). "Don't worry about it" (alarming). Be specific about which framework and how.

## 4. "If something an AI sends goes sideways — wrong promise, missed disclosure, anything — who's liable?"

**Short answer.** Same liability stack as if your producer sent it manually. The producer's name is on the message; the agency holds the supervisory responsibility; we provide the infrastructure. AI doesn't change the chain of liability — it changes the chain of *speed and consistency*. That's why we make supervisor review of AI drafts a default-on option for any agency that wants it.

**If they press.** We carry tech-E&O insurance to cover platform-level failures (data loss, system errors). We do *not* carry producer-level E&O — that stays with the producer and the agency, as it always has. The pilot agreement makes this explicit. In practice, the templates that ship pre-approved are conservative by design: no guaranteed returns, no specific premium quotes in nurture, no jurisdiction-bypassing language. The biggest liability reduction is actually from the audit trail — when there's a complaint, you can pull the exact message in seconds rather than reconstructing it from screenshots.

**Don't say.** "We're liable." (We're not, and the lawyers will catch that.) "Just trust the AI." (Nobody trusts AI in regulated comms.)

## 5. "Has any other financial services agency used this yet?"

**Short answer.** Not yet — GFI would be the first MLM financial services agency. We have ~3,400 active real estate agents using the underlying platform, so the AI nurture, CRM, billing, and infrastructure are battle-tested. The financial-services vertical layer is new, and that's exactly why we're proposing a pilot rather than an enterprise contract — to build it *with* you.

**If they press.** Being first has two upsides for GFI: (1) the vertical gets built around your workflow and your compliance posture, not a competitor's; (2) the case study at the end becomes a moat — every agency that comes after sees GFI's name on the outcome data. Real-estate-agent CRM is a solved category; the AI-driven MLM-financial-services CRM doesn't exist yet, and the agency that helps build it gets first-mover positioning.

**Don't say.** "We have lots of customers." (Be honest; they'll check.) "It's basically the same as real estate." (It's not, and that's why they need a custom vertical.)

## 6. "What if our top producers don't adopt it?"

**Short answer.** Top producers don't need it — they've already built their machine. The pilot deliberately targets producers in the 30–80% percentile, where the lift is largest. If we move *their* speed-to-lead from hours to minutes, and their FNAs-per-week from 1 to 4, the override revenue going up the hierarchy moves more than top-producer numbers would.

**If they press.** That said, top producers will pay attention if (a) the FNA looks better than what they generate manually, and (b) the recruit pipeline view helps them see their downline without a spreadsheet. We let them opt in on their own pace. The pilot success metrics specifically don't depend on top-producer adoption. If a top producer ignores the platform during the 90 days, the metrics still move if the cohort works as designed.

**Don't say.** "Your top producers will love it." (You don't know that yet, and execs see through it.) "It'll automate them out of a job." (Top producers aren't worried about being automated; they're worried about losing their personal touch.)

## 7. "What stops WFG, Primerica, or another competing agency from using this against us?"

**Short answer.** Two things. First, the pilot — we can write 90-day exclusivity within the MLM financial-services vertical if that's important to you. Second, post-pilot, GFI is in the case study and in the product. The features we build for GFI's pilot become GFI's competitive advantage *because they're shipped first against your workflow*. We're happy to make the exclusivity question concrete in the commercial terms.

**If they press.** Realistically, the platform will eventually be available to others — that's how SaaS scales. The way GFI stays ahead is by being first, by helping shape the product, and by being the agency with measurable outcome data. If exclusivity matters more than first-mover advantage, we can structure a longer carve-out (12 months in the vertical) in exchange for revenue commitments or co-marketing. We can negotiate either direction.

**Don't say.** "We'd never sell to WFG." (You shouldn't promise that — it's a bad business commitment.) "Exclusivity is impossible." (It isn't — it's a commercial term, and they know that.)

## 8. "What if Transamerica builds this themselves?"

**Short answer.** Transamerica is a $35B insurance carrier — they're not in the business of building producer-facing CRMs. They've had 20 years to build a producer AI tool and haven't, because their incentive is to ship policies, not to optimize the pre-sale workflow. That's why agencies like GFI exist — to do the work the carrier doesn't.

**If they press.** Even if Transamerica decided tomorrow to build something, three things would still be true: (1) they'd build for *all* their affiliated agencies, not for GFI specifically — generic by definition; (2) carrier-built tools historically lag in UX by 3–5 years; (3) anything Transamerica builds is carrier-locked, so GFI couldn't use it to recruit producers who carry appointments with other carriers. Our platform is carrier-neutral by design — which is what an agency that wants flexibility actually needs.

**Don't say.** "They'd never compete with us." (They might; don't promise.) "We're irreplaceable." (Nothing in software is irreplaceable.)

## 9. "Where does the data live? Can we get it out if we leave?"

**Short answer.** Supabase (Postgres), US-region, encrypted at rest and in transit. GFI owns the data — at any point during or after the pilot, you can export everything to CSV/JSON in a single click. We don't lock data behind tiers or charge for export.

**If they press.** Specifically: prospects, contacts, recruits, FNAs, messages, calendar events, recordings — all exportable. PII is handled per standard SaaS practice. We're SOC 2 Type II in progress; we can share the current security posture document if your CISO or compliance team wants to dig in. For agencies that require self-hosted, we can quote a private deployment, but it's significantly more expensive and not what we'd recommend for a pilot. If you ever want to leave, we provide a 30-day transition window with full export and read-only access while you migrate.

**Don't say.** "Your data is safe." (Vague; everyone says that.) "We're SOC 2." (Don't claim certifications you haven't completed.)

## 10. "What does pricing actually look like after the pilot if we want to expand?"

**Short answer.** Per-producer monthly: $49 individual, $39 at 10+ seats (Agency tier), custom at 200+ or for white-label. No setup fees, no long contracts, month-to-month after the pilot. Pilot pricing is free for the 90 days; post-pilot pricing is what every other agency pays.

**If they press.** Pricing is illustrative until contract — for an agency at GFI's potential scale, the commercial structure could be (a) per-seat subscription, (b) flat agency license with unlimited producers, (c) revenue share on premium written through the platform, or (d) white-label with a one-time setup fee plus per-seat. Each has trade-offs around upfront cost, alignment of incentives, and lock-in. We typically recommend (a) for the first year, then move to (b) or (d) as the producer base stabilizes. We're flexible — what matters is that the structure makes both sides want to keep selling.

**Don't say.** "We'll figure it out later." (Execs read "we don't have it together.") "It's negotiable." (Without anchoring to a number, this signals weakness.)

## Bonus: MLM-specific questions to be ready for

### B1. "How does this plug into our hierarchy and override commission structure?"

**Short answer.** The platform models a recursive agent hierarchy day-one — every producer has an upline, and every upline sees their downline's pipeline activity. Override commission tracking isn't in the pilot scope (we'd integrate with carrier-provided commission statements in phase 2), but the hierarchy data structure is built to support it. For the pilot, the upline view answers "what is my downline doing" — that alone is something most MDs do with spreadsheets today.

**If they press.** Post-pilot, we ingest your commission statement CSVs (from Transamerica, Nationwide, Foresters, etc.), parse per-producer commissions, and apply your override schedule by hierarchy tier. The agent-facing dashboard shows earned overrides, advanced overrides pending chargeback, and downline-driven revenue. We can demo the data model on the recruit pipeline page today — production commission ingestion is a phase-2 deliverable.

### B2. "Does it support Spanish, Mandarin, Vietnamese — our producers serve those communities?"

**Short answer.** Yes. The AI generates SMS, email, FNAs, and call scripts in 12+ languages including Spanish, Mandarin (simplified and traditional), Vietnamese, Korean, and Tagalog — your producer just picks the language per prospect. Compliance disclosures are translated and reviewed by language. Bilingual AI is in every tier from Producer up.

**If they press.** Translation isn't just text — it's idiom and tone. We've tuned the prompts so a Spanish FNA reads like a Spanish-language financial advisor wrote it, not like Google Translate handled it. For Mandarin specifically, simplified and traditional are separate templates because the audiences are different. If your team serves a language we don't yet support, adding one takes about a week.

## Closing posture

Execs don't expect perfect answers. They expect:
1. **Honest acknowledgment** that the concern is real.
2. **A specific, narrow answer** (not "we're great").
3. **Visible thinking** — show you've considered it before they raised it.

If you don't know an answer: *"I don't know — let me come back to you within 48 hours with a specific answer."* That earns more credibility than a hand-wave.

**The one question you can't dodge:** if they ask whether you can name another financial services agency using this — be honest that GFI would be the first. The follow-up is the value of being first.
