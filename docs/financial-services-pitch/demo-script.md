# Demo Script — GFI Executive Walkthrough

> **Audience:** 1–2 senior GFI execs, informal setting.
> **Target time:** 12 minutes of demo + 3–5 minutes of conversation = 15–17 minutes total. Tight on purpose.
> **Format:** Live walkthrough of the GFI-themed workspace, no slides, no deck.
> **Ask at the end:** A verbal yes to scope a pilot with one MD's team. Not a contract.
>
> The script is structured as scenes with **timing**, **navigation**, **talk track (verbatim-friendly)**, and **what to point at**. Don't read it word-for-word — internalize the beats, use your own voice.

---

## Pre-demo setup (15 minutes before the call)

1. **Open the GFI-themed workspace** in a clean browser window:
   - `https://leadsmart-ai.com/financial-services` (landing — start here)
   - Pre-sign-in on a second tab to `https://leadsmart-ai.com/financial-services/dashboard/overview`
   - Pre-load `https://leadsmart-ai.com/financial-services/dashboard/fna` (so the form is already filled with Diana Alvarez's facts)
   - Pre-load `https://leadsmart-ai.com/financial-services/one-pager` as the leave-behind
2. **Pre-generate the FNA once** before the call by hitting "Generate FNA" — that warms the cache, so during the live demo it returns in <2 seconds instead of 60.
3. **Close everything else.** Slack, email, notifications. Quit them, don't just mute. Execs notice toast popups and it kills the moment.
4. **Browser zoom 110–125%** — your laptop screen is small for a demo. Bigger text reads on Zoom.
5. **Have a notepad open** for any objections or commitments they make — write them down visibly.

## Scene 0 · 30 sec · The hook (before opening anything)

**Don't open the screen yet.** Speak first.

> "Before I show you anything, here's the bet. Your producers spend more time on things software should be doing — chasing inbound leads back, building FNAs in PowerPoint, tracking their downline in a spreadsheet — than they spend in front of clients. The producers who fix that grow. Most don't.
>
> We built a workspace that gives them that time back. Fifteen minutes — let me show you the four pieces that move the needle, then I'll tell you what we're proposing."

**Then share screen.**

## Scene 7 · 90 sec · The ask

**Navigate:** Either stay on the dashboard, or open the one-pager tab.

**Stop sharing screen.** Look at them. This is a conversation, not a slide.

**Talk track:**

> "Here's what we're proposing. A 90-day pilot. One MD's team — you pick who. 10 to 25 producers. Platform is free for the pilot cohort, no setup fees, no contract.
>
> We measure four numbers against your baseline: speed-to-lead, FNAs per producer per month, recruit interest to licensed at 60 days, premium submitted by new producers.
>
> At day 90 we sit down, look at the numbers, and write a one-page decision: expand, extend, or exit. We commit to one vertical-specific feature per month based on your cohort's feedback during the pilot.
>
> The next step is a 30-minute call with whichever MD you'd want to pilot this with. We scope the cohort, share baselines, lock the timeline. Nothing signed today."

**Then stop. Don't fill the silence.** Let them respond.

## Q&A branches — anticipate these questions

### "How is this different from Transamerica's tools / TransACT / AgencyBloc / Salesforce FSC?"

> "Carrier tools handle the post-application side — illustrations, e-app, policy admin. We don't touch any of that. Your producers keep using carrier portals.
>
> AgencyBloc and Redtail were built for traditional independent agents — no recruit hierarchy, no AI nurture, no FNA generator. Salesforce FSC is too heavy and too expensive for field producers.
>
> We sit in the pre-sale layer and the recruiting layer. The gap nobody else fills."

### "What about compliance / TCPA / FINRA?"

> "Every outbound message is opt-in audited — we have proof-of-consent rows for every SMS we send. The communications archive is built for the spirit of 17a-4 retention. AI-drafted messages can be queued for principal review before send. State disclosures auto-append by jurisdiction.
>
> The pilot week 1 work is your principal reviewing our template library — one-time effort, then producers can send freely."

### "Has anyone else in our space used this?"

> "Not yet. GFI is the first MLM financial services agency. That's by design and that's the offer — first means the product gets shaped around your workflow, not a competitor's, and the case study at the end becomes a moat for you."

### "What stops WFG / Primerica from using this?"

> "Two answers. One, the pilot — we can write 90-day exclusivity within the MLM financial services vertical into the agreement if that matters to you. Two, post-pilot, the case study is yours. The agencies that come after see GFI's outcome data — that's the first-mover advantage."

### "What's the catch on the free pilot?"

> "No catch. We're early in this vertical and we need real-world data from an agency that runs at scale. You get 90 days of platform access. We get honest metrics and a case study. If the pilot fails, we walk away."

### "What does pricing look like after the pilot?"

> "Per-producer monthly: $49 individual, $39 at 10-plus seats. No setup fees. Month-to-month after the pilot. For a GFI-scale rollout, we'd structure something custom — per-seat, flat agency license, revenue share, or white-label all on the table. Designed so both sides want to keep selling."

### "When does it go live?"

> "Two weeks from agreement signed. Week 1: compliance template review with your principal. Week 2: cohort onboarding and first AI nurtures going out."

## Closing posture

If they say **"let's do it" / "send me the next step"** — confirm: *"I'll have the scoping doc in your inbox within 24 hours. Who's your principal so we can loop them in for the compliance review?"*

If they say **"let me think about it"** — confirm: *"Totally fair. Want me to send a follow-up with the one-pager, the FAQ, and the pilot scope in writing — or wait until you've had time?"*

If they say **"we need to talk to legal/compliance first"** — confirm: *"Smart. I'll send you a one-pager on the compliance posture so they have what they need."*

If they say **"not for us"** — *"Appreciate the time. Mind if I ask what the deal-breaker was?"* Then write down what they say. That's the most valuable thing you'll hear all week.

---

## Things NOT to do in the demo

- **Don't show pricing during the demo.** Pricing kills exploratory conversations. Save it for the follow-up.
- **Don't apologize for "Coming soon" items.** Frame them as roadmap funded by the pilot.
- **Don't open multiple tabs / get lost in navigation.** One linear path.
- **Don't read the screen out loud.** They can read. Talk *about* what they're seeing.
- **Don't pile features.** Six scenes max. Anything beyond that is for the working session.
- **Don't promise dates you don't control.** "Pilot week 2" is a commitment we can keep. "Live in January" is one we can't.
